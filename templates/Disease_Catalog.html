Disease Catalog

  1. Common Cold: A viral infection that causes symptoms like a runny or stuffy nose, sneezing, and a sore throat.
  2. Influenza (Flu): A contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses, leading to fever, cough, and body aches.
  3. Allergic Rhinitis (Hay Fever): An allergic reaction to pollen, dust, or other allergens, causing symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes.
  4. Acne: A skin condition characterized by the development of pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads.
  5. Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD): A chronic condition where stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing heartburn and other symptoms.
  6. Urinary Tract Infection (UTI): An infection in any part of the urinary system, leading to symptoms like frequent urination and a burning sensation.
  7. Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS): A gastrointestinal disorder that can cause symptoms such as abdominal pain, bloating, and changes in bowel habits.
  8. Eczema (Dermatitis): A chronic skin condition characterized by inflammation, itching, and the development of a rash.
  9. Chickenpox: A highly contagious viral infection causing an itchy rash and flu-like symptoms.
  10. Headache Disorders (e.g., Migraine, Tension Headache): Conditions characterized by head pain and other associated symptoms.
  11. Hypertension: High blood pressure, often leading to heart disease and stroke.
  12. Diabetes Mellitus: A chronic condition that affects how the body processes glucose (sugar) in the blood.
  13. Cancer: A group of diseases characterized by uncontrolled cell growth and the potential to spread to other parts of the body.
  14. Asthma: A chronic respiratory condition that causes difficulty in breathing due to narrowed airways.
  15. Alzheimer's Disease: A progressive neurodegenerative disease leading to memory loss and cognitive decline.
  16. Parkinson's Disease: A neurological disorder that affects movement and can cause tremors and muscle stiffness.
  17. Arthritis: Inflammation of the joints, leading to pain and decreased mobility.
  18. Osteoporosis: A condition in which bones become weak and brittle, increasing the risk of fractures.
  19. Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD): A group of lung diseases, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema, that restrict airflow.
  20. HIV/AIDS: A viral infection that attacks the immune system, making it difficult for the body to fight off infections.
  21. Malaria: A mosquito-borne infectious disease causing fever, chills, and flu-like symptoms.
  22. Tuberculosis (TB): A bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs but can spread to other parts of the body.
  23. Hepatitis: Inflammation of the liver, typically caused by viral infections (e.g., Hepatitis A, B, C).
  24. Epilepsy: A neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures.
  25. Stroke: A sudden interruption of blood flow to the brain, leading to brain damage and functional impairments.
  26. Multiple Sclerosis (MS): An autoimmune disease that affects the central nervous system, causing a range of symptoms.
  27. Rheumatoid Arthritis: An autoimmune disease that primarily affects the joints, leading to pain and swelling.
  28. Lung Cancer: A type of cancer that originates in the lungs, often caused by tobacco smoking.
  29. Heart Disease: A range of conditions that affect the heart's structure and function, including coronary artery disease and heart failure.
  30. Cystic Fibrosis: A genetic disorder that affects the lungs and digestive system, leading to mucus buildup and respiratory problems.
  31. Lupus: An autoimmune disease that can affect multiple systems in the body.
  32. Gout: A type of arthritis caused by the buildup of uric acid crystals in the joints.
  33. Crohn's Disease: An inflammatory bowel disease that can affect the entire digestive tract.
  34. Ulcerative Colitis: An inflammatory bowel disease that primarily affects the colon and rectum.
  35. Sickle Cell Anemia: A genetic blood disorder that causes red blood cells to become misshapen and break down.
  36. Endometriosis: A painful condition in which tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows outside the uterus.
  37. Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): A hormonal disorder that affects the ovaries and can lead to various health issues.
  38. Ovarian Cancer: A type of cancer that affects the ovaries.
  39. Huntington's Disease: A genetic disorder that leads to the progressive breakdown of nerve cells in the brain.
  40. Myasthenia Gravis: A neuromuscular disorder that causes muscle weakness and fatigue.
